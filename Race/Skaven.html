Skaven

Source Warhammer Fantasy
Skaven are a clan-based society in which the number 13 holds important significance. Skaven are potrayed as sneaky, conniving, selfish, cowardly and backstabbing. They are also closely linked t plague and disease. The Skaven believe themselves to be superior to all other races, especially those that live above ground.

Age: With a lifespan of about 20 years, asserting that, were it not for the (often violent) internal competition of their species, they might live well past 50. More powerful embers of Skaven tend to live far longer, even hundreds of years, due to the use of drugs or magic.

Alignment and Religion: Chaotic. It should be noted that due to these views and their tendency for back-stabbing, no one (not even the minions of Chaos) ever want to count the Skaven as their allies. Everyone who knows about the Skaven knows that there is only one rule for trusting a Skaven: don't. All Skaven pay homage to the Horned Rat, their only deity, whom they worship primarily out of fear of retribution.

Skaven Racial Traits

Your skaven character has a variety of natural abilities the result of thousands of years of mutations.

+2 Dexterity: Your Dexterity score increases by 2.
Skaven: Skaven are monstrous humanoids with the skaven subtype.
Medium: Skaven are roughly man-sized, the typical specimen being between four and five feet tall on average. Your size is medium and have no bonuses or penalties due to size.
Normal Speed: Your base speed is 30 feet.
Senses: Skaven get the Scent special ability, Low-Light Vision and Darkvision with a range of 60feet. Accustomed to dark caves and subterranean tunnels, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shares of gray.
Born Coward: You take a -2 penalty on saves against fear.
Plagueborn: You take a +2 bonus on saves against poison.
Languages: Skaven begin play speaking Common and Undercommon. Skaven with high Intelligence scores can choose any languages they want (except secret languages, such as Druidic).

Subraces

Skaven society is split between various clans and factions. Most notable of these ancient clans are the Warlock-Engineers of Clan Skyre, the fanatical Plague-Monks of Clan Pestilen, the Assaassin-Adepts of Clan Eshin, the mutated War-beast of Clan Moulder and the Military Stormvermin of clan Mors.

Clan Skyre

+1 Intelligence: Your Intelligence score increases by 1.
Spell-Like Ability: A Clan Skyre Skaven can use theAcid Splash cantrip at-will as a spell-like ability (with a caster level equal to the skaven's character level.)
When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the Ray of Sickening spell once per day.
When you reach 5th level, you can also cast the Stinking Cloud spell once per day.
Superior Darkvision: Your darkvision has a radius of 120 feet.
Light Sensitivity: Skaven are dazzled in areas of bright sunlight or within the radius of a daylight spell.

Clan Pestilen

+1 Constitution: Your Constitution score increases by 1.
Plague Aura: You can use your standard action to spread your plague. When you use your plague aura, each creature in a 5 foot radius must make a Fort saving throw. (DC = 10 + 1/2 your level + your Constitution modifier) A creature takes 1d4 poison damage on a failed save and is poisoned until the end of their next turn.The damage increases to 2d4 at 6th level, 3d4 at 11th level, and 4d4 at 16th level.
After you use your plague aura ability, you can't use it again until after an hour.

Clan Eshin

+1 Charisma: Your Charisma score increases by 1.
Sneaky: You can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist and other natural phenomena. Can use stealth in partial concealment, partial cover, dim light.
Scurry: Your base land speed increases to 40 feet.

Clan Moulder

+1 Wisdom: Your Wisdom score increases by 1.
Speak with Rats: Through simple squeaks and gestures, you can communicate simple ideas with rats, wererats and other ratkin.
Pack Master: As a leader among vermin, a Swarm of Rats follow you around answering to your beck and call. The swarm acts independently of you, but it awlays obeys your commands. In combat, it rolls its own initiative and acts on its own turn. The swarm can't attack, but it can take other actions as normal.
When the swarm drops to 0 hit points, all the rats in the swarm are dead. You can spend 1 hour and 10gp to find enough rats for a new swarm.

Clan Mors

+1 Strength: Your Strength score increases by 1.
Vicious Fangs: You are never unarmed. You are proficient with your fangs, which are a melee weapon that deals 1d6 piercing damage. Once per turn, if this attack hits a creature that is your size or smaller, you may attempt to grapple the target.
